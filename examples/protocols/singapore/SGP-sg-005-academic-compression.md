# Protocol SG-005: The Academic Compression Base Rate

> **Source**: r/SGExams (u/privatealvlsg)
> **Context**: Private A-Level Candidate (10 Months -> >85RP)
> **Purpose**: Calibrating the *actual* vs. *perceived* difficulty of credential acquisition.

---

## 1. The Base Rate Arbitrage

**The Public Belief (The Illusions):**

* **Timeframe**: "You need 2 years of intense school support."
* **Result**: "Self-study is impossible/suicide."
* **Safety**: "If you miss a week, you fall behind."

**The Private Reality (The Base Rate):**

* **Timeframe**: 10 months is sufficient. (Actually ~6.5 months effective work).
* **Result**: >85 RP (Local Uni Entry) to 90 RP is achievable.
* **Buffer**: Falling off for 3.5 months (35% of timeline) is recoverable.

**The Insight**: The Singapore education system is designed for the *mean* student (low agency, low strategy). For a *strategic* agent, the efficiency gains differ by ~300%.

* *School*: 2 years x Bureaucracy = 1 Credential.
* *Arbitrage*: 6.5 months x Strategy = 1 Credential.

## 2. Selection Strategy (The Lever)

Success is determined at *Subject Selection*, not Exam Day.

| Subject | Difficulty to Self-Study | Note |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Sciences (Phy/Chem)** | 🔴 Hard | Requires labs, complex concepts. Avoid. |
| **History** | 🟠 Medium | Heavy content/memorization. Doable but time-costly. |
| **Economics (H2)** | 🟡 Medium | Concept-heavy but hackable via Answer Keys. |
| **MOB (Business)** | 🟢 Easy | "Straightforward." High ROI. |
| **POA (Accounting)** | 🟢 Easy | "Logic-based." High ROI. |
| **Math (H1)** | 🟢 Easy | Drillable. |

**The Strategy**: Don't fight the "Hard" war (Sciences). Fight the "Smart" war (Business/Accounting/Econs).

* *Note*: H2 POA used to count as a Math subject. (Check current syllabus).

## 3. Tactical Alpha (The "Secret" Syllabus)

**1. The Source Material Trick**

* **Avoid**: Carousell "Premium" Notes, UK A-Level Books (Irrelevant context).
* **Use**: **MI (Millennia Institute) Notes**.
  * *Why*: MI teaches 3-year A-Levels to slower students. Their notes are structured for *comprehensibility* and *completeness*. They are the gold standard for self-study.

**2. The Reverse-Engineering Protocol**

* **Inefficient (Student)**: Read Textbook -> Understand Theory -> Try Question -> Fail.
* **Efficient (Hacker)**: Read Answer Key (TYS/Prelim) -> Deconstruct Logic -> Apply to Question.
* *Mechanism*: For H2 Econs, reading 50 answer scripts reveals the "Pattern Language" of the exam board better than any textbook.

## 4. Psychological Defense

**The "Too Late" Fallacy**:

* *Trigger*: "I haven't studied in 2 weeks. I'm screwed."
* *Reality*: OP lost 3.5 months (health/personal) and still scored >85RP.
* *Rule*: **It is never too late until the paper starts.** The timeline is elastic.

**The "Expert" Fallacy**:

* *Trigger*: "My teacher/friend said X is impossible."
* *Reality*: Most advice is projection of the advisor's limitations.
* *Action*: Look for the *Positive Deviant* (the 1 guy who did it) and model them. Ignore the mean.
