# Agree and Reassert Protocol

## Date Added: 10 December 2025

> **Related Protocol**: 40-frame-vs-structural-problem (Frame Problem fix category)  
> **Session Origin**: Internal session log (freeloading-client case)

## 6.1 The Trap Flow

You want to set a boundary, but original script is "confrontational" rather than "assertive":

```
Your original script: "What you did was really uncool/shady..."
└─ Focus: on HIS behaviour/morality
└─ Leaves space for debate

GTO script: "When... happened, I felt really annoyed/disappointed..."
└─ Focus: on YOUR feelings
└─ Cannot be debated
```

**Trap Flow Diagram:**

```
You accuse → He deflects ("But we booked tickets ages ago...")
         → You repeat accusation
         → He escalates ("You're crazy/too sensitive")
         → Explosion (-100)
```

## 6.2 GTO Formula: The Triple

| Step | Function | Example |
|------|----------|---------|
| 1. Validate his "reason" | Let him lower his shield | "I hear you — you did book tickets in advance." |
| 2. Reassert your feeling | Nail the boundary | "I just want you to know, I genuinely felt pretty crappy being ditched like that." |
| 3. Exit | Don't let -10 escalate to -100 | "That's all." |

## 6.3 Complete Script Example

**Scenario:**

You: "Mate, honestly, you ditching me for your girlfriend's trip — I was pretty annoyed."

Him: "But we booked tickets ages ago — you're overthinking it."

**Your GTO Response (Perfect Pivot):**

> "I hear you — you did book tickets in advance. I just wanted to let you know, I genuinely felt pretty crappy being ditched like that. That's all."

## 6.4 Why This Is Invincible

| Component | Function |
|-----------|----------|
| "I hear you..." | Disarms immediately — he can't fight facts |
| "I genuinely felt..." | Irrefutable — he can argue intent, but not your feelings |
| "That's all." | Clear topic end — no room for debate |

## 6.5 Comparison Table

| Your Original Script | Recommended Script |
|---------------------|-------------------|
| Focus: His moral wrong | Focus: Your internal feelings |
| Goal: Force him to admit fault | Goal: Express feeling + set boundary |
| Result: Argument + "Why do you keep repeating" | Result: Point made + topic naturally moves on |
| Intensity: Like -50 (courtroom trial) | Intensity: Like -10 (normal feedback) |

## 6.6 Core Insight: "Being Heard" vs "Getting an Apology"

💡 Don't get trapped in needing him to "plead guilty" — that's your -100 trauma demanding "justice." A healthy -10 just wants to be heard. Say your piece, nod at his excuse (but don't accept it), then walk away with swagger — that's real power.
