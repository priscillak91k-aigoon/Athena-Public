# Spindle Buffer Protocol (5-10 Second Delay)

## Date Added: 10 December 2025

> **Related Protocol**: 28-three-second-override (same neuroscience basis)

## 1.1 Core Metaphor

Restaurant kitchen workflow:

```
Waiter writes order → Spikes it on spindle (queue)
Chef takes orders in sequence → Cooks or rejects
No arguing with paper — binary decision: do or don't
```

The spindle is a **buffer** — orders don't get processed immediately, they queue.

## 1.2 The Problem: Real-Time Response = Explosion

```
OLD MODE (No Buffer):
Stimulus → Must respond immediately
    ↓
Brain needs 5-10s to process
    ↓
Pressure / freeze / emotional flooding
    ↓
Attack / meltdown output
    ↓
Relationship / money / reputation damaged
```

## 1.3 New Mode: Spindle Protocol

```
Stimulus → Spike on Spindle (queue)
    ↓
Say "Let me get back to you"
    ↓
Process on YOUR timeline (minutes to hours)
    ↓
Compose with full brain (prefrontal)
    ↓
Send calm, calculated response (or no response)
```

## 1.4 Neuroscience Basis

| Mode | Dominant Brain Region | Outcome |
|------|----------------------|---------|
| Immediate pressure | Amygdala (fight/flight) | Emotional, impulsive, regretful |
| With buffer | Prefrontal (thinking/planning) | Calm, strategic, precise |

**Why 5-10 seconds is critical:**

```
Amygdala hijack duration: ~6 seconds
Prefrontal back online: ~10 seconds

If you respond within 10 seconds → Amygdala dominates → Emotional output
If you wait 10+ seconds → Prefrontal takes over → Rational output
```

## 1.5 Execution Scripts

| Scenario | Standard Script |
|----------|-----------------|
| Client causing trouble | "Let me check and get back to you." |
| Being accused / conflict | "I hear what you're saying. Let me gather my thoughts and respond." |
| Outrageous request | "I'll need to look at our policies on this." |
| Emotions triggered | "I need a moment to cool down. Let's continue later." |
| WeChat voice bombardment | "Can't take calls now — can you text?" |
| Face-to-face confrontation | "Let me think about how to respond to this." |

💡 **Key**: Make them *feel heard* while buying yourself buffer time.

## 1.6 Common Resistance and Responses

| Resistance | Response |
|------------|----------|
| "Why can't you answer now?" | "I want to give you a proper response, not a rushed one." |
| "It's a simple question" | "I need to think it through. Will get back to you." |
| Internal feeling: "This is rude" | A delayed response is 100× more polite than an emotional explosion |
