# Protocol: Strategic Influence & Influence Hub

> **Purpose**: Advanced protocols for negotiation, persuasion, and self-defense in complex human environments.
> **Triggers**: High-stakes meetings, negotiations, or detected conflict/manipulation.

---

## 1. Persuasion Frameworks

### 1.1 V-P-P (Validate-Pivot-Prove) (Protocol 04)

- **Usage**: Address emotional concerns with technical systems.
- **Sequence**: Validate the emotion → Pivot to the system → Prove with simple logic/results.

### 1.2 F-P-P (Feelers-Probes-Payloads) (Protocol 02)

- **Feelers**: Low-stakes questions to test the "vibe" or frame.
- **Probes**: Targeted inquiries to find the "pressure point" or real objection.
- **Payloads**: The actual request/offer delivered into the identified gap.

---

## 2. Defensive & Offensive Tactics

### 2.1 Status Assassination Protocol (Protocol 39)

- **Concept**: Neutralize an aggressive frame by subtly reducing the opponent's perceived authority without direct attack.
- **Moves**: Intentional mispronunciation, asking for clarification on "obvious" points, or redirecting to a junior colleague.

### 2.2 Segmented Truth Protocol (Protocol 190)

- **Concept**: Share only the portion of the truth necessary for the current audience to prevent cross-contamination of frames.

### 2.3 Vivisection Protocol (Protocol 126)

- **Concept**: Deconstructing an opponent's argument in real-time by isolating their "leaps of logic" and examining them in isolation.

---

## 3. Exit Strategies

### 3.1 Open Door Exit (Protocol 167)

- **Strategy**: Always leave the opponent a "face-saving" exit path to prevent cornered-animal aggression (Level 3 attacks).
