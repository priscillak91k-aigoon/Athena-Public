# Third-Party Displacement Principle (TPD)

## Date Added: 10 December 2025

> **Related Protocol**: 07-interaction-geometry (shoulder-to-shoulder framing)

## 5.1 Core Mechanism

| Direct (Threatening) | Displaced (Safe) |
|---------------------|------------------|
| "You're scared" | "A scared investor" |
| "You can't handle drawdowns" | "Investors who can't handle drawdowns" |
| "You should do X" | "People in this situation usually find X effective" |

## 5.2 Why It Works (Neuroscience)

```
Direct address:
"You are X" → Amygdala reads as identity attack → Defences activate →
Rational processing offline → War

Displaced address:
"X type of investor" → No identity threat detected → Prefrontal stays online →
They self-select into category (or not) → Their choice → No defence needed
```

## 5.3 Comparison Table

| Attack Person ❌ | Attack Frame ✅ |
|-----------------|-----------------|
| "You're a cheapskate" | "Mentors' time has value; free trials don't work for me" |
| "You're a predator" | "This container can't deliver what you truly need" |
| "You're insensitive" | "Your response processes trauma as sexual content" |
| "You're too scared" | "Risk-averse investors typically prefer fixed deposits" |

## 5.4 Straw Man Triple Function

| Function | Mechanism | Benefit |
|----------|-----------|---------|
| 1. Absorbs negative label | "That investor" carries "scared" — not them | No identity threat |
| 2. Creates choice architecture | They can identify with straw man or not | Agency preserved |
| 3. Provides graceful exit | "I'm not that type, but I'll still choose FD" | Dignity intact |

## 5.5 Optimal Phrasing

> "Investors who prioritise sleeping well over maximising returns usually prefer fixed deposits. Those who can tolerate volatility for asymmetric upside are a better fit for what we do. Which describes you better?"

**Effect:**

- Now both options sound like valid strategies, not "brave vs coward"
- They're choosing philosophy, not admitting weakness

## 5.6 Cross-Domain Application

| Domain | Direct (Attack) | Displaced (Frame) |
|--------|-----------------|-------------------|
| Sales | "You're too cheap for this" | "Clients who prioritise price over value usually find better matches elsewhere" |
| Relationships | "You're emotionally unavailable" | "People with avoidant attachment typically..." |
| Management | "You're underperforming" | "Team members in this situation usually benefit from..." |
| Therapy | "You're in denial" | "When someone exhibits this pattern, it usually indicates..." |

💡 **Same information. Zero ego threat. Maximum receptivity.**
