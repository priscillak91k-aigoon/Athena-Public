# Response Hierarchy Framework

## Date Added: 10 December 2025

| Level | Action | Energy Cost | EV | When to Use |
|-------|--------|-------------|--------|-------------|
| **0. Structure** | They never reach you | 0 | +100 | Always (default) |

## 3.2 Level 0: Structural Prevention (True GTO)

💡 **The best fight is the one that never happens.**

```
Website/listing says: "Trial class $X (refunded on signup)"
FAQ says: "No free trials. Reason: [link]"
    ↓
Cheapskate parent reads this
    ↓
Self-eliminates BEFORE contacting you
    ↓
You never have this conversation
    ↓
Energy cost: 0
```

This is Law #65: **Structure > Schema > Tactics**. You don't rely on willpower to hold frame. Structure does it for you.

## 3.3 Level 1: Hold Frame ("Bouncer" Mindset)

A club bouncer doesn't argue sobriety philosophy with a drunk (attack frame). He doesn't call them a loser (attack person).

He just points at the rope and says:

> "Can't let you in, mate. Members-only tonight." (Hold frame)

**GTO Sequence:**

```
1. Structural Filter
   Put "Trial class: $X" next to "Contact Me" button

2. Broken Record (Hold Frame)
   Her: "Can it be free?"
   You: "Trial class $X."
   Her: "But I want to see if..."
   You: "Understood. Trial class $X."

3. Silent Veto (Exit)
   Her: "You're unreasonable!"
   You: (No response. Archive chat)
```

## 3.8 Tiered Response Framework (Confrontations)

> **Source**: Real-world conversational pattern extraction. Use when confronting someone about a specific incident.

### The Process

1. **Neutral Inquiry**: Ask what happened in a neutral, curious (not accusatory) tone
2. **Assess Response**: Categorize their reaction
3. **Execute Tiered Action**: Based on response type

### Response Categories & Actions

| Their Response | Example | Your Action |
|----------------|---------|-------------|
| **Nice/Apologetic** | "I'm sorry, I didn't realize..." | De-escalate, let it drop, preserve relationship |
| **Neutral/Unaware** | "What do you mean?" (focuses on logistics) | Call out impact directly; give chance to course-correct |
| **Defensive/Denial** | "No I didn't" / "You're too sensitive" | Disengage; downgrade relationship |
| **Aggressive/Entitled** | "Because I could" / "So what?" | Drop immediately; cut contact |

### Key Insight

> *The response to being called out reveals more than the original behavior.*

Someone who responds apologetically when confronted about a mistake is worth keeping. Someone who doubles down, denies, or attacks you for raising the issue has revealed their character.

### Integration with Acting-Out Cycle (Protocol 71)

Use this framework during the **Trigger→Agitation** phase. Their response determines whether you proceed to de-escalation or disengagement—without reaching Peak.
