About Unix Timestamps

A Unix timestamp (Epoch time) is the number of seconds since January 1, 1970 00:00:00 UTC. It's widely used in programming, databases, APIs, and logging systems because it's timezone-independent and easy to compute with. Millisecond timestamps (13 digits) are also supported.
