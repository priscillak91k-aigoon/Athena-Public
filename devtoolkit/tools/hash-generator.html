About Cryptographic Hashes

Cryptographic hash functions produce a fixed-size output (digest) from any input. They're used for data integrity verification, password storage, digital signatures, and checksums. SHA-256 is the most widely used for security applications. MD5 and SHA-1 are considered cryptographically broken but still used for checksums.
