🐕 Quinn — Noise Sensitivity & Avoidance Training

Quinn's Profile

10 months old. Sensitive to noises. Afraid of new people and dogs. Avoidant (non-aggressive). This is a fear-based behavior, not disobedience.

The Method: DSCC (Desensitization + Counter-Conditioning)

Desensitization

Expose Quinn to the scary stimulus at such a low intensity she doesn't react. Gradually increase over days/weeks.

Counter-Conditioning

Pair the scary thing with something she loves (high-value treats, favorite toy). Goal: scary thing → "oh good, treats!"

Step-by-Step Protocol for Quinn

Step Action Key Rule
1 Identify specific triggers (which noises, which situations) Be precise — "loud trucks" not just "noises"
2 Find her threshold — the distance/volume where she notices but doesn't react If she refuses treats, you're too close/loud
3 Play trigger sounds at very low volume via phone speaker Start absurdly quiet
4 Immediately give high-value treats when sound plays Treats PREDICT the sound, sound doesn't predict treats
5 Increase volume by tiny increments over sessions 5-15 min sessions, 1-2x daily
6 If she shows ANY fear (shaking, panting, hiding, won't eat), go back a step NEVER push through fear
7 Generalize to different environments once comfortable Dogs don't generalize well — redo in new places

For People/Dog Fear

  • Distance = safety. Start exposure from across the street, not face-to-face.
  • Treat her when she sees the person/dog (before she reacts)
  • Let HER choose to approach. Never force.
  • Ask strangers NOT to reach toward her or make eye contact initially
  • Calm, confident body language from you — she reads your stress
⚠️ Critical Rules:
  • NEVER punish fear — it makes it worse, always
  • Comforting is OK — contrary to old advice, comforting during fear does NOT reinforce it
  • Provide a safe space — a crate or quiet room she can retreat to voluntarily
  • If symptoms are severe, consult a veterinary behaviorist (not just a trainer)

Recommended Resources (Dunedin)

  • YouTube: search "DSCC dog training noise sensitivity" for visual demos
  • App: Sound Proof (Puppy) — has pre-loaded desensitization sounds
  • Treats: Use something Quinn goes crazy for (cheese, liver treats, chicken)

💰 NZ Tax Obligations for Lobotto Prints

The Short Version

Question Answer
Do I need to register as a sole trader? Yes, if you're earning income. Use your personal IRD number.
When do I register for GST? When revenue hits $60,000 NZD in any 12-month period
GST rate? 15% on NZ sales. 0% (zero-rated) on international sales.
Do I pay income tax? Yes — on net profit (revenue minus expenses)
What expenses can I deduct? Etsy listing fees, subscription costs, equipment, internet (portion)
