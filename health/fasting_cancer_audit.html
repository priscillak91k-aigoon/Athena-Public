The Base Mechanics: mTOR, AMPK, & IGF-1

Cancer is not a mystical disease; it is a metabolic engineering failure. Cells forget how to die, and they hijack your fuel lines to replicate indefinitely. Fasting manipulates three specific metabolic levers to force a hostile environment for tumor growth.

1. IGF-1 (The Growth Signal)

Insulin-like Growth Factor 1 tells cells to multiply. High IGF-1 is heavily correlated with breast, prostate, and colon cancers. Fasting tanks IGF-1 levels. Without this signal, pre-cancerous cells stall out and fail to replicate.

2. mTOR (The Engine)

Mammalian Target of Rapamycin is the cellular engine for protein synthesis and growth. Overactive mTOR = cancer. Fasting shuts mTOR down, shifting the cell from "build mode" to "repair mode."

3. AMPK (The Cleanup Crew)

AMP-activated Protein Kinase is the body's low-fuel sensor. Fasting activates AMPK, which triggers Autophagy (the cell eats its own damaged proteins and organelles). This clears out the junk that causes DNA mutations.

4. Differential Stress Resistance

Healthy cells adapt to fasting by hunkering down and protecting their DNA. Cancer cells, driven by broken mutations, cannot adapt. They starve and undergo apoptosis (cell suicide) while healthy cells survive.
