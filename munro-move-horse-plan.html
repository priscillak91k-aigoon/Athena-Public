🔗 Harness & Hitching Setup

Equipment Needed

  • 2× Full draught harnesses — collar/hames style preferred over breastcollar for heavy pulls
  • 2× Sets of traces (chains or heavy straps from harness to singletree)
  • 2× Singletrees (horizontal bars that balance the pull on each horse's shoulders)
  • 1× Doubletree (evener bar) — connects both singletrees and equalises pull between the two horses
  • 1× Tow chain or heavy strap — links the doubletree to the Munro's tow hitch/drawbar
  • Breeching on both horses — allows them to hold back if the caravan rolls forward

How It Connects

  1. Each horse wears their harness (collar + hames + breeching + traces)
  2. Each horse's traces attach to their individual singletree
  3. Both singletrees attach to the doubletree (evener bar)
  4. The doubletree connects via chain/strap to the Munro's tow ball or drawbar
  5. Person 1 leads from the front with long reins
⚠️ Critical: Use a proper doubletree, not just a rope or chain. Without the evener bar, one horse takes more load than the other → sore shoulders, uneven pull, and a caravan that veers sideways.

📋 Move-Day Execution

🌅 Pre-Move (Morning — 7-8 AM)

  • Confirm 4 people: 1 horse handler, 1 caravan guide, 2 ply shufflers
  • Bring horses to site early — let them settle and walk the route once unburdened
  • Check all harness buckles, traces, singletrees, and doubletree connections
  • Drop Munro tyre pressure to 20–25 psi
  • Raise jockey wheel fully up and locked
  • Lay first 2 ply sheets end-to-end under caravan wheels (4.8m runway)

🐴 The Move

  1. Hitch up: Back horses into position. Connect traces → singletrees → doubletree → Munro drawbar.
  2. Check alignment: Horses should be walking on grass BESIDE the ply, not on it.
  3. Signal to walk: Steady, slow walk. No trotting. Smooth continuous pull.
  4. Leapfrog: As caravan rolls forward, 2 people grab rear ply sheet → run it to the front → lay it down.
  5. Repeat until Munro reaches canopy spot.
⚠️ If a horse balks or refuses: STOP. Don't force it. Horses sense ground instability — if they won't go, the ground may be too soft. Reassess the route.

🏁 Final Positioning

  1. Unhitch horses BEFORE final micro-adjustments.
  2. Lower jockey wheel. Position Munro by hand using jockey wheel.
  3. Place concrete pavers under tyres (×2), corner steadies (×4).
  4. Place 4×2 timber under jockey wheel.
  5. Re-inflate tyres. Deploy steadies. Level. Chock wheels.

🛡️ Horse Safety Notes

  • Distance: This is a very short pull (10-20m max). Well within any horse's capacity with 2 animals.
  • Ground: Horses handle soggy Dunedin grass far better than any vehicle. Their hooves distribute weight naturally.
  • Breeching: Essential — if the caravan rolls forward on a slight slope, the breeching lets the horses hold it back safely.
  • Never tie traces directly to the drawbar — always use a singletree/doubletree setup so the pull balances across each horse's shoulders.
  • Keep the path obstruction-free: No garden hoses, wire, or anything on the grass that could tangle hooves.
  • Cool-down: After the pull, walk the horses for 5-10 minutes before putting them away.
