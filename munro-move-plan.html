🪵 Plywood Specs (Validated)

15mm CD Structural Ply — untreated is fine (temporary use, not permanent).

  • Why 15mm, not 17mm? — The Munro is sub-900kg. Each tyre carries ~225kg max. 15mm structural CD is rated for this. Saves ~$4/sheet and is lighter to manhandle.
  • Why structural, not reject? — Reject/seconds grade has internal voids and delamination risk. Under concentrated tyre point-loads on soggy ground, one bad spot = the sheet snaps and the wheel punches through into mud. Not worth saving $30/sheet.
  • Why not 12mm? — Would flex under load and let mud swallow the edges.

🔁 The 3-Sheet Leapfrog Method

  1. Sheet 1 & 2: Lay under the Munro's wheels (one per wheel track, or side-by-side if access is narrow)
  2. Sheet 3: Place ahead of the van as the "next step"
  3. Quad tows the Munro forward onto Sheet 3
  4. Grab Sheet 1 from behind → leap it to the front
  5. Repeat until you reach the canopy spot
  6. Final position: Slide the 4×2 timber under the jockey wheel as a permanent pad

Jockey wheel trick: Don't waste a full sheet on it. Let the jockey wheel ride on the edge of whichever sheet the left tyre is on.

If the run is under ~7m: Lay all 3 sheets end-to-end (3 × 2.4m = 7.2m) — no leapfrogging needed at all.

📋 Move-Day Execution

  • Book delivery for morning of move day (7–8 AM while ground has overnight firmness)
  • Drop Munro tyre pressure to 20–25 psi (increases footprint, grips the ply)
  • Lay the first 2 sheets under the Munro's wheels
  • Place Sheet 3 ahead, begin leapfrog
  • One person on the Quad, one person guiding jockey wheel + shuffling sheets
  • If Quad spins on ply — STOP. Reposition. Don't force it.
  • DO NOT snatch-pull with a 4WD — 1970s steel drawbar will bend
  • Once in position, place 4×2 timber under jockey wheel
  • Re-inflate tyres to normal pressure
  • Stack ply sheets against fence (reusable)
