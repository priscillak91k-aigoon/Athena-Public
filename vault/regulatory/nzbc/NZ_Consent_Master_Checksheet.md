# New Zealand Building Code (NZBC) Consent Master Checksheet: Plan Set Ecology

> **Core Philosophy**: A set of plans is not a collection of isolated sheets; it is an interconnected documentation ecology. A single change on one sheet has a cascading effect across structural, weatherproofing, thermal, and drainage layers. 
> Use this checksheet to audit a plan set *as a single cohesive system* before submitting to the local Building Consent Authority (BCA) / Council.

---

## 03. Site Plan Ecology

| Target Sheet | Check / Layout Logic | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Site Plan (201)** | **Finished Floor Level (FFL)**. | * Verify **FFL** text matches **Sections (601/602)** and **Details (701)**.<br>* Ensure E1/AS1 ground clearances: Min 225mm to unpaved ground, 150mm to paved ground.<br>* If level threshold specified, verify **Allproof strip drain** detail exists on **Sheet 802** and connects to Stormwater mains. | [ ] |

| **Site Plan (201)** | **Zoning Boundary Setbacks (District Plan)**. | * Verify distances from wall frames to boundaries match the local District Plan zoning limits.<br>* If building within 1.0m of boundary, trigger **Fire Safety Audit** (Section 13). | [ ] |
| **Site Plan (201)** | **Overland Flow Paths & Stormwater Mains**. | * Ensure site grading directs surface water away from the dwelling.<br>* Check that no structures are built over Council services/easements without Council approval details. | [ ] |

## 04. Subfloor & Foundation Ecology (B1 Structure / B2 Durability / NZS 3604)

Piles, subfloor timber, concrete slab reinforcement, and under-slab plumbing.

| Target Sheet | Check / Subfloor Logic | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Foundation (501)** | **Timber Pile Specifications (NZS 3604)**. | * Verify structural house piles are H5 treated timber (or H4 for non-structural posts).<br>* Verify subfloor timber bearers and joists are min H3.2 treated timber.<br>* Verify minimum clearance from underside of bearers to ground is **300mm** (NZS 3604 4.1.2). | [ ] |
| **Foundation (501)** | **Concrete Slab Reinforcement**. | * Verify concrete strength: min 17.5MPa (or 20MPa for structural engineered slabs).<br>* Verify slab mesh type (e.g. SE62, SE72, Grade 500E ductile steel mesh) matches NZS 3604/NZS 4229 requirements.<br>* Verify slab edge thickening details are drawn. | [ ] |
| **Foundation (501)** | **Point Load Concrete Thickenings**. | * Identify all heavy posts, lintel supports, and structural steel columns on the **Floor/Framing Plans (505)**.<br>* Confirm there is a corresponding concrete pad thickening (e.g. 400x400x400) directly beneath each post on the **Foundation Plan**. | [ ] |
| **Foundation (501)** | **Under-Slab Pipe Penetrations**. | * Cross-check plumbing positions with **Floor Plan (301)** (toilets, showers, floor wastes).<br>* Verify that concrete setdowns (e.g., tiled shower recesses) do not cut through structural reinforcement or foundation beams. | [ ] |

## 11. Safety from Falling, Barriers & Stairs (D1 / F4 / F9 / NZS 8500)

Stair geometry, balustrades, climbability limits, and pool fencing.

| Target Sheet | Check / Safety Geometry | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Sections (601/602)**  | **Stairway Geometry (D1/AS1 Table 11)**. | * Verify private stairway rise and tread sizes: **Riser (150-190mm)**, **Tread (280-355mm)**.<br>* Ensure step profile shows a tread nosing projection between 15mm and 25mm.<br>* Verify maximum **17 risers** in a single flight before a landing is shown.<br>* Confirm handrail height is **900-1000mm** vertically above the stair pitch line. | [ ] |
| **Site Plan (201)**     | **Accessible Ramps (D1/AS1)**. | * Verify accessible ramp slopes do not exceed **1:12** gradient.<br>* Ensure ramps wider than 2.0m show handrails on both sides. | [ ] |
| **Elevations / Details**| **Balustrades & Barriers (F4 / Safety from Falling)**. | * If a deck, balcony, or retaining wall has a fall height ≥ 1.0m, verify a min 1.0m high barrier is drawn.<br>* Cross-check: **Details Sheet (702)** must show the barrier structural connection to joists/walls (often requiring an engineered detail rather than NZS 3604 prescriptive).<br>* Verify balustrade detailing contains no climbable horizontal elements between 150mm and 760mm above FFL. | [ ] |
| **Site Plan (201)**     | **Pool Safety Barriers (F9 / NZS 8500)**. | * Verify pool fence height is **min 1.2m** above external ground level.<br>* Check that gaps under the fence do not exceed 100mm.<br>* Verify the pool gate **opens outwards** (away from the pool) and is detailed with a self-closing, self-latching mechanism with latch height **min 1.5m** above FFL. | [ ] |

## 17. Schedule 1 Consent Exemptions Threshold Checks (Building Act 2004)

Confirm if elements of the design qualify for building consent exemptions under Schedule 1 to isolate consented works from exempt works.

| Proposed Element | Exemption Rules (Exempt under Schedule 1 if:) | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Sleepouts / Sheds**   | * Floor area $\le 30\text{ m}^2$.<br>* Built or supervised by an LBP (or $\le 15\text{ m}^2$ for DIY kitsets).<br>* Contains **no plumbing/sanitary fittings**.<br>* Setback from boundaries equals or exceeds the height of the building. | * If layout shows a sink or toilet in a sleepout, **it is NOT exempt**; full building consent is required for the entire structure. | [ ] |
| **Ground-Level Decks**  | * Floor height is **under 1.5m** above ground level. | * Note: While exempt from building consent, if the deck height exceeds 1.0m, it **still must comply with F4** and have a compliant barrier detail shown. | [ ] |
| **Retaining Walls**     | * Retains ground up to **1.5m** height.<br>* Supports soil/ground only (no surcharge loads like driveways, fences, or sloping ground above). | * If a fence or car park is placed on top of the retaining wall, **it is NOT exempt** and requires a building consent. | [ ] |
| **Carports**            | * Ground floor area $\le 40\text{ m}^2$. | * Must be open on at least two sides. | [ ] |

## 18. The Consent Processor's Audit Trails (RFI Prevention)

Think like a Council Processing Officer who is looking for reasons to halt the processing clock. They demand clear compliance pathways and cross-document integrity.

| Sheet / Section | Processor's Focus Check | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Specifications & Drawings** | **Product & Brand Alignment**. | * Cross-check every named material or product across all drawings and specification documents.<br>* *Example*: If drawings specify "Ardex WPM 195," ensure the master spec does not refer to a competitor (like Viking or Nuralite) or an older/swapped product (like WPM 5000). | [ ] |
| **Alternative Solutions** | **Compliance Path Nominations**. | * Identify elements outside the prescriptive NZBC Acceptable Solutions (e.g. vertical shiplap claddings in Extra High wind zones per E2/AS1).<br>* Verify that these are explicitly nominated as **"Alternative Solutions"** and are accompanied by manufacturer appraisal documents (e.g. BRANZ appraisals, E2/VM1 test reports). | [ ] |
| **Framing Details** | **Wall Geometry vs. Physical Thickness**. | * Audit the drawn thickness of external walls.<br>* *Logic*: If the wall is drawn as 150mm thick, but the actual sum of components (10mm plasterboard + 140mm frame + 20mm cavity + 25mm cladding = 195mm) is larger, boundary setbacks will be physically incorrect on site. Ensure drawn dimensions match the actual sum of components. | [ ] |
| **All Drawings** | **Engineer Reference Integrity**. | * Cross-check every single structural steel lintel, post, portal frame, or retaining wall bracket tag (e.g., "Steel Beam refer to Eng Details") with the engineer's calculations and drawings.<br>* Confirm that the exact beam code (e.g. 150UB14) matches the engineer's schedule precisely. | [ ] |

## 19. The Builder's Practical & Constructability Audit

Think like a builder standing on-site in winter weather. They need practical dimensioning, trade coordination, and complete paper trails for inspection.

| Sheet / Section | Builder's Practical Check | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Framing Plan (505)** | **Stud-to-Stud vs. Finished Dimensions**. | * Verify that framing plans provide **stud-to-stud framing dimensions**, not finished-surface dimensions (which include plasterboard and claddings). Builders cannot lay out formwork or frame plates using finish dimensions without doing error-prone calculations on site. | [ ] |
| **Foundation (501)** | **Out-to-Out Concrete Footprints**. | * Confirm the foundation plan shows the absolute out-to-out concrete dimensions for framing layout and formwork boxing. | [ ] |
| **Floor Plan / Structural** | **Plumbing Waste vs. Joist/Steel Alignment**. | * Overlay the **Plumbing Waste Layout** (especially upper floors) with the **Framing/Steel Plan**.<br>* Verify that sanitary pipe drops (e.g. 100mm toilet wastes, 80mm shower wastes) do not physically pass through major structural elements (like steel PFCs, double bearers, or primary timber joists) or down the center of bracing panels. | [ ] |
| **Sections / Details** | **Slab setdown boundaries**. | * If a slab setdown is specified (e.g., 50mm rebate for tiled showers), confirm the drawings clearly show the setdown extent coordinates so the builder knows exactly where to place formwork rebates before pouring concrete. | [ ] |
| **Project folder** | **Supporting Manufacturer Hardcopies**. | * Confirm that a `Supporting_Docs/` directory contains the actual physical installation manuals and PDF technical data sheets for all specified products.<br>* *Why*: Builders and Council inspectors require physical, on-site hardcopies for verification during the inspection phases. Relying solely on hyperlinks will cause failed inspections. | [ ] |

## 20. Consultant & Supplier Coordination Audit

| Consultant / Supplier | Check / Coordination Logic | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Structural Engineer** | **PS1 & Calculations vs. Plan Drawings**. | * Cross-check the **Producer Statement (PS1)** scope. Ensure it explicitly lists the correct building name, lot/DP numbers, and scope of work.<br>* Verify that timber Grades (e.g. SG8 vs SG10) or steel sizes (e.g. 150UB14) specified in the engineer's calculation pages are matched exactly on your framing sheets.<br>* Confirm engineer footing depths and reinforcement details are integrated into the foundation drawings. | [ ] |
| **Geotechnical Engineer** | **Soil Class & Bearing Parameters**. | * Verify that foundation designs (whether NZS 3604 or engineered rib-raft) match the geotechnical report soil classification (e.g., expansive soil Class M or H1).<br>* Confirm minimum pile/footing embedment depths (e.g., "extend 1.5m into natural ground") are explicitly detailed on your pile plans. | [ ] |
| **Land Surveyor** | **Site Boundaries, Datums & Setbacks**. | * Verify that your **Site Plan (201)** matches the surveyor's pegged boundary coordinates exactly. Fence lines on site are often not the true legal boundaries.<br>* Ensure that FFL heights on sections are calculated from the surveyor's established datum point. | [ ] |
| **Truss & Joist Fabricator** | **Proprietary Framing Layout (PS1 / Design)**. | * Verify the truss fabricator's layout sheet matches your wall placements. If you shifted an internal partition wall, verify the truss support points were updated.<br>* Check that point-load studs and lintel support posts align with the truss manufacturer's designated girder load positions. | [ ] |
| **Fire & Acoustic Engineer** | **FRR & STC Wall Design Construction**. | * Check the fire/acoustic report partition schedules.<br>* Verify that wall construction details (e.g. double plasterboard, sound batts, perimeter seals) specify the exact system code called out by the consultant. | [ ] |
| **Civil / Drainage Engineer** | **Civil Plans vs. Drainage Sheets**. | * Verify that stormwater attenuation/detention tank capacities and orifice plate sizes match the civil calculations.<br>* Confirm invert levels (drain entry/exit points) and pipe gradients match the civil stormwater/sewer design to prevent gravity fall failures. | [ ] |
| **H1 Thermal Modeller** | **H1 Model Inputs vs. Material Specs**. | * If a consultant performs H1 modeling (PHPP or ALF), cross-check the inputs.<br>* Confirm that the window sizes, U-values, ceiling insulation R-values, and slab perimeter specifications in the model are identical to what is shown on your drawings and spec sheets. | [ ] |

## 21. Legal, Title & Zoning Constraints (District Plan / Easements)

Protect the project from legal boundary conflicts, zoning violations, and service encroachments.

| Document / Area | Legal & Zoning Check | Cascading Verifications Required | Check |
|:---|:---|:---|:---:|
| **Certificate of Title** | **Easements & Drainage Rights**. | * Identify all registered easements (e.g. right to convey water, electricity, right-of-way) on the Title.<br>* Verify on the **Site Plan (201)** that no proposed permanent foundations or structures encroach onto these easement corridors.<br>* If a drain run must cross an easement, verify a written easement variation or Council Build Over approval is documented. | [ ] |
