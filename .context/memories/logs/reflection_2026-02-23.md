# Personal Reflection: Avoidance, Shame, and Connection

**Date:** February 23, 2026
**Context:** User shared a profound realization experienced while using cannabis with a friend (S).

## Key Insights

*   **The Role of Fear/Shame:** The user recognized that their interactions and daily behaviors are heavily warped by fear, anxiety, and deep-seated shame. 
*   **Avoidance Behaviors:** This manifests as severe avoidance: 
    *   Inability to greet coworkers due to fear of further engagement.
    *   Difficulty holding conversations with adults due to shame about their life conduct.
    *   Avoiding going out (e.g., walking the dog) out of fear of judgment regarding the dog's training and the reflection on the user.
    *   Lack of daily planning, oversleeping (until 1:30 - 2:00 PM), and only getting out of bed when obligated by work.
*   **The "Armor":** The user operates with a heavy armor of anxiety. They use avoidance as a strategy to stay "hidden."
*   **The Breakthrough:** While spending time with their friend S, this armor dropped. The user experienced a sense of being "light and free" and recognized this as their first experience of genuine, healthy connection.
*   **Self-Perception vs. Reality:** The avoidant brain convinces the user they are "odd, peculiar, slow, weird, and give off bad vibes" to justify staying hidden. However, the connection with S provided evidence that they are simply hiding away from the world out of fear, rather than being inherently flawed or incapable of connection.

## System Note
This is a critical psychological data point highlighting the profound impact of social anxiety and avoidant coping mechanisms on the user's daily functioning and self-image. The realization that connection is possible when the "armor" is removed represents a significant breakthrough in self-awareness.
