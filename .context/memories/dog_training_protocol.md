# Training Protocol: Quinny (Border Collie)

> **Goal**: Establish basic manners, mental stimulation, and a clear communication method for going to the toilet from the main house (breaking the caravan association).
> **Rule of Thumb**: Border Collies need a "job." 15 minutes of intense mental work is often more exhausting for them than a 1-hour run. Keep sessions to 5-10 minutes, 1-2x a day.

---

## 🐶 Phase 1: Solving the "Outside" Confusion (Bell Training)

Since you live between the caravan and the main house, Quinny suffers from **State-Dependent Learning**. She knows how to ask to go outside *when in the caravan*, but doesn't generalize that to the main house. We need a physical, literal trigger.

**The Solution: Dog Potty Bells**
1. Buy a cheap string of bells and hang them on the main house door handle.
2. **Step 1:** Every single time you take her out to the toilet from the house, say "Touch", take her paw/nose, physically ring the bell, then immediately open the door and praise her.
3. **Step 2:** Wait by the door. Say "Touch." If she sniffs or nudges the bell on her own, jackpot praise and open the door.
4. **Step 3:** Over the next 2-3 weeks, she will realize "Ringing this bell makes the big wooden door open to the potty area."

---

## 🧠 Phase 2: Daily Manners & Obedience (10 Mins/Day)

Integrate these into her 1-hour walk or during the "Wake & Prep" afternoon block.

### 1. The "Wait" Command (Impulse Control)
*Border Collies are highly reactive to movement. Teaching impulse control is critical.*
* **Meals:** Never let her eat until you say "Okay." Make her sit and wait while you put the bowl down.
* **Doors:** She must sit and "Wait" before going through *any* door (house, caravan, car). You walk through first.

### 3. "Leave It" (Disengagement)
*Vital for safety (dropping dangerous items) and ignoring distractions on walks.*
* Put a low-value treat in your closed fist. Let her sniff. Say "Leave it."
* The second she pulls her nose away and looks at you, reward with a *better* treat from your other hand.

---

## 🏃‍♀️ Phase 3: Free Time Blocks

If you find yourself with an open "Free Time" block, here are high-value activities for a Border Collie:
1. **The Second Walk:** Another 30-45 minutes of physical exertion.
2. **Sniffari (Decompression Walk):** Take her somewhere new on a long leash and just let her sniff for 30 minutes. (Sniffing lowers heart rate and exhausts them mentally).
3. **Hide and Seek:** Hide her favorite toy or treats around the house/yard and tell her to "Find it!"
