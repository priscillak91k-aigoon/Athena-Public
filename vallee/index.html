WHO IS JACQUES VALLÉE

Jacques Vallée is not a crackpot. He's a computer scientist who worked on ARPANET (the precursor to the internet), an astronomer who catalogued Mars with NASA at JPL, and a venture capitalist. He is also the only person in UFO research that the US government has consulted seriously on multiple occasions. He was the inspiration for the French scientist character in Close Encounters of the Third Kind. Spielberg chose him because he represented the serious side of the field.


His thesis, developed over 50 years, is not that UFOs are fake. It is that the conventional explanation — interplanetary spacecraft piloted by biological beings — is demonstrably wrong. And he proves it with math.

WHY IT CAN'T BE SPACECRAFT
  • Sightings have occurred at a rate impossible to explain by interstellar travel logistics
  • Craft violate known physics — right-angle turns at hypersonic speeds, no sonic boom
  • Occupant reports describe beings that evolved alongside human mythology (faeries, demons, angels — same functional description, different cultural costume)
  • Encounters frequently cause lasting psychological transformation, not radiation sickness
  • The phenomenon interacts differently in response to human belief systems — it shapeshifts
  • No physical hardware has ever been independently verified
THE CONTROL SYSTEM THEORY

Vallée argues the phenomenon functions as a control system — something that deliberately regulates human belief and behaviour by presenting itself at the edge of credibility. Always enough evidence to be taken seriously. Never enough to be proven.


It has done this across every major civilisation. The content changes. The structure doesn't. Medieval peasants reported encounters with glowing aerial craft piloted by beings from "the land below." We call them flying saucers. The encounter mechanics are identical.


The control system is not necessarily malevolent. It may be instructional. The question is: who — or what — is doing the teaching?
